How it works

From blank page to compliant product

Plain English explanations at every step. No compliance experience required.

1
Register your product
Choose your toy type: 3D printed, crochet, soft/sewn, or wooden. MakerSafe tailors everything to match.
2
Work through the wizard
Spec, materials, manufacturing, risk assessment, EN71 testing. Everything covered, tailored to your toy type.
3
Record results & evidence
Log pass/fail for each EN71 test. Attach photos, lab reports, or certificates directly against the test they cover.
4
Export your documents
Declaration of Conformity, Technical File, batch labels, and swing tags. One click bundles everything into a compliance pack ZIP.

EN71 safety testing

Every applicable test. Nothing more.

MakerSafe shows only the EN71 tests relevant to your product type and age grading. Each test card explains exactly what equipment you need, how to perform the test, and what counts as a pass.

Document generation

Professional documents, ready in seconds

Everything required under TSR 2011 Schedule 5, generated from the data you've already entered.

📄
Declaration of Conformity
A properly formatted DoC citing the correct standards (EN71-1, EN71-2, EN71-3), your company details, signatory, and declaration date. Auto-generated from your wizard data. Just download and sign.
📋
Technical File
Full Schedule 5 bundle covering product spec, BOM, manufacturing, risk assessment, test records, and age grading. Everything Trading Standards may ask for.
🏷️
Batch Labels & Swing Tags
UKCA-compliant labels and compact swing tags with UKCA mark, age warning, lot number, standards line, and manufacturer address. Print-ready PNGs, one per batch.
📦
Compliance Pack ZIP
One download containing your DoC PDF, Technical File PDF, all batch labels and swing tags, and all uploaded evidence files, organised by test category.

Built to the letter of UK law

Every document output — Declaration of Conformity, Technical File, and batch labels — maps directly to the requirements of the Toys (Safety) Regulations 2011 (S.I. 2011/1881).

🏛️

Developed with local authorities

We're working with Trading Standards officers at several local authorities to make sure what MakerSafe produces is in line with what they actually expect to see from small manufacturers.

🔍

Honest about its limits

MakerSafe is a documentation tool, not a rubber stamp. It tells you clearly what it does and doesn't cover, and won't let you generate documents without the evidence to back them up.
