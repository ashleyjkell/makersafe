Why this matters

UKCA compliance isn't optional

The Toys (Safety) Regulations 2011 apply to every toy sold in the UK, however it's made or sold. Here's what's at stake if you skip it.

⚖️
It's a legal requirement
Under the Toys (Safety) Regulations 2011, placing a non-compliant toy on the UK market is a criminal offence, regardless of whether you're a sole trader selling two items a month or a large business. There is no minimum scale threshold. Event organisers and market holders can also carry liability if they knowingly allow non-compliant goods to be sold at their events, so many are now asking sellers for documentation before letting them trade.
🛒
Platforms and markets are enforcing it
Etsy actively enforces toy safety compliance and can remove listings or suspend accounts without it. Local markets and craft fairs are increasingly requiring a Declaration of Conformity before allowing toy sellers to trade. Trading Standards have the power to issue recalls, confiscate stock, and impose fines. A DoC is the first thing they ask for.
🚫
"It's a decoration" doesn't work
Labelling something as a "display item", "decoration", "prop", or "not a toy" does not make it exempt. If it looks like a toy and a child would play with it, it is legally treated as a toy under TSR 2011. Trading Standards can and do prosecute regardless of how the item is described or labelled. The exemption test is how a child would use it, not what the seller calls it.
🤝
It builds buyer trust
Stockists, gift shops, and wholesale buyers increasingly require a DoC before placing orders. The UKCA mark and a proper Technical File show customers and inspectors that you take safety seriously, and give parents confidence in what they're buying for their children.
💡

Already selling without compliance? MakerSafe helps you get compliant from today. You can't backdate evidence, but you can start building a proper Technical File right now and protect yourself going forward. Many of our users started exactly where you are.

Common questions

Before you sign up

The questions we hear most often from makers who are new to UKCA compliance.

Do I really need this if I only sell a few toys a month?
Yes. The Toys (Safety) Regulations 2011 have no minimum quantity threshold. Placing even a single non-compliant toy on the UK market is an offence. The law applies equally to a sole trader selling at two craft fairs a year and a business selling thousands of units.
I've been selling without compliance — what do I do now?
MakerSafe helps you get compliant from today. You can't retroactively create evidence for past sales, but you can document your process properly going forward and start building a Technical File for each product. Many makers start here. The important thing is that you act.
Can I perform EN71 tests myself, or do I need a laboratory?
It depends on the test. Many EN71-1 physical and mechanical tests — tension, torque, bite force — can be performed by a manufacturer with basic equipment. MakerSafe explains what you need and how to do each one. EN71-3 chemical migration (heavy metals) typically requires an accredited laboratory. MakerSafe flags which tests normally need external lab support.
Is a Declaration of Conformity I make myself legally valid?
Yes. TSR 2011 places the responsibility for conformity on the manufacturer — that's you. A DoC is a self-declaration, not a third-party certificate. What matters is that the testing and technical documentation behind it is genuine and retained for 10 years. MakerSafe generates a correctly formatted DoC and helps you build the supporting evidence.
What if Trading Standards inspect my stall or my online listings?
They will ask for your Declaration of Conformity and, in more serious cases, your Technical File. MakerSafe generates both. Keep a digital copy on your phone and consider printing a DoC to bring to markets. A complete, professional Technical File demonstrates you take compliance seriously.
What product types does MakerSafe cover?
Currently: 3D printed toys, crochet and knit toys, soft/sewn toys, and wooden toys. Each type has its own tailored set of EN71 tests, material library categories, and manufacturing sections. Other product types are on the roadmap — sign up to be notified when they're added.
